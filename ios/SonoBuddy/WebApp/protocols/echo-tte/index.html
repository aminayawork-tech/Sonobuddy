Transthoracic Echocardiogram (TTE)

advanced 45–60 min· Phased array cardiac transducer (1–5 MHz)

Patient Position

Left lateral decubitus for parasternal/apical views. Supine for subcostal. Left arm raised to open intercostal spaces.

Indications

Dyspnea, chest pain, heart failure evaluation, murmur, valve disease, LV function, pre-op cardiac clearance

1

Parasternal Long Axis (PLAX)

Left sternal border, 3rd–4th intercostal space. Indicator toward right shoulder. Identify LV, LA, aortic root, LVOT, mitral valve, and pericardium. Confirm cylindrical LV without foreshortening.

Phased array, cardiac mode

Tips

→

LV should look cylindrical — if foreshortened, slide probe one space up or down

→

Normal aortic root: ≤4.0 cm (men), ≤3.7 cm (women) at sinus of Valsalva

→

Pericardial effusion: anechoic stripe posterior to LV — measure largest dimension

2

PLAX — M-Mode LV Measurements

Place M-mode cursor at mitral valve tip level. Measure IVSd, LVIDd, LVIDs, LVPWd at end-diastole (Q wave) and end-systole (minimum cavity).

Tips

→

Fractional shortening (FS) = (LVIDd − LVIDs) / LVIDd — normal >28%

→

Teichholz EF = 7.0/(2.4 + LVIDd) × LVIDd³ − LVIDs³ — use when windows are limited

→

Wall thickness: IVS + LVPW >25 mm = severe hypertrophy

3

Parasternal Short Axis (PSAX) — Multiple Levels

Rotate 90° clockwise from PLAX. Sweep from base to apex: aortic valve level ("Mercedes-Benz" tri-leaflet), mitral valve level ("fish mouth"), papillary muscle level, and apex.

Tips

→

AV level: assess all 3 leaflets symmetrically — bicuspid AV has only 2 leaflets with a midline raphe

→

MV level: evaluate anterior (long, reaches posterior wall) vs posterior (short) leaflet, assess for MVP or commissural fusion

→

PM level: "doughnut" LV — assess all 11 segments (wall motion, hypertrophy, thinning)

4

Apical 4-Chamber (A4C)

Probe at cardiac apex, indicator toward left. LV on right, RV on left of image. Both AV valves at same horizontal level. Evaluate LV/RV size, LA/RA size, AV valves, interatrial and interventricular septa.

Tips

→

True apex must be at top — if LV looks short, you are foreshortening

→

RV should be ≤2/3 the size of LV — RV enlargement = RV strain, PE, PHTN, RV MI

→

Color Doppler: sweep across MV and TV to grade regurgitation severity

5

Apical 2-Chamber (A2C) & Biplane EF

From A4C, rotate 60° counterclockwise. Only LV and LA visible. Trace endocardial borders in both A4C and A2C for biplane Simpson's LVEF — the most reproducible EF method.

Tips

→

Simpson's biplane: trace diastolic and systolic endocardium in A4C and A2C

→

Include papillary muscles in cavity (trace around them)

→

Normal LVEF ≥55%; mildly reduced 41–54%; severely reduced ≤40%

6

Apical 5-Chamber — LVOT Doppler

Tilt probe anteriorly from A4C to bring LVOT and aortic valve into view. PW Doppler at LVOT: record VTI (velocity-time integral) for stroke volume. CW Doppler through AV for peak/mean gradient.

Tips

→

LVOT VTI × LVOT area (π × r²) = stroke volume — normal VTI 18–22 cm

→

AV peak velocity >4 m/s = severe AS; mean gradient >40 mmHg = severe AS

→

DI (dimensionless index) = LVOT VTI ÷ AV VTI — <0.25 = severe AS

7

Mitral Inflow & Tissue Doppler (Diastology)

PW Doppler at mitral leaflet tips in A4C: measure E-wave, A-wave, E/A ratio, E deceleration time. Tissue Doppler (TDI) at septal and lateral mitral annulus: measure e' velocity. Calculate E/e' ratio.

Tips

→

E/A <0.8 + DT >200 ms = Grade I diastolic dysfunction (impaired relaxation)

→

E/e' >14 = elevated LV filling pressure

→

Indeterminate diastology: ≥2 of 4 criteria positive → Grade II

8

Subcostal Views & IVC

Patient supine, probe in epigastrium. Subcostal 4-chamber (RV nearest transducer). Rotate 90° for IVC long-axis. Measure IVC diameter and collapsibility index with sniff maneuver.

Tips

→

IVC <1.7 cm + >50% collapse = RAP 0–5 mmHg (hypovolemic/euvolemic)

→

IVC >2.1 cm + <50% collapse = RAP ≥10 mmHg (elevated RA pressure)

→

Subcostal is best view for pericardial effusion assessment and tamponade
