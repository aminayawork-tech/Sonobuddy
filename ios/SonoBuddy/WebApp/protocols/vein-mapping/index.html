Vein Mapping (Pre-Access / CABG)

intermediate 30–60 min· High-frequency linear 7–15 MHz

Patient Position

Legs slightly dependent for LE mapping. Arm slightly elevated for UE. Use tourniquet above mapped segment to distend veins. Warm room improves visualization.

Indications

Pre-CABG bypass (saphenous conduit evaluation), pre-dialysis access creation (AV fistula/graft planning), pre-peripheral bypass

1

Patient Setup & Vein Preparation

Warm the room, apply tourniquet above the area being mapped to distend veins. Begin with a quick survey (color Doppler) to confirm overall patency before detailed mapping.

Tips

→

Tourniquet above area maps the functional diameter under dialysis needle tension

→

Warm room (22°C+) and warm patient = better vasodilation and larger vein diameters

→

Prepare a washable marking pen — you will mark the skin as you scan

2

GSV — Lower Extremity Mapping

Start at saphenofemoral junction. Track GSV medially down the thigh, across the knee, and into the calf. Measure diameter (inner wall to inner wall) transversely at proximal thigh, mid-thigh, above knee, and mid-calf.

Tips

→

Minimum GSV diameter for CABG: ≥3.0 mm (some surgeons accept 2.5 mm)

→

Minimum for peripheral bypass: ≥3.0–3.5 mm

→

Mark skin with pen at each measured level — draw the course on the skin

3

SSV & Accessory Saphenous Veins

Short saphenous vein: posterior calf from lateral malleolus to popliteal fossa. Also evaluate any accessory saphenous systems. Document diameter, course, and any varicosities.

Tips

→

SSV joins popliteal vein at saphenopopliteal junction — location can vary (high vs low insertion)

→

Accessory saphenous (anterior) may be a viable conduit if main GSV is inadequate

→

Varicosities: marked dilation, tortuous, usually unsuitable as conduit

4

Cephalic & Basilic — Upper Extremity Mapping

If UE veins are requested (dialysis access planning): cephalic from wrist to cephalic-axillary junction, basilic from wrist to basilic-axillary junction. Measure diameter at wrist, forearm, antecubital, upper arm.

Tips

→

Minimum cephalic/basilic diameter for AVF creation: ≥2.5–3.0 mm

→

Basilic vein is deeper and may require transposition for dialysis needling

→

Cephalic arch: tight curve joining axillary — document this junction carefully

5

Depth Measurement & Final Map

Document depth (skin surface to anterior vein wall) at each marked level. Create a labeled map of vein courses, diameters, and depths on a body diagram. Mark skin with pen at vein locations.

Tips

→

Depth >8–10 mm: difficult for dialysis needling — may need surgical deepening

→

Very superficial veins: easier access but more fragile for bypass

→

Document any anatomical variants: duplicated GSV, bifurcations, absent segments
